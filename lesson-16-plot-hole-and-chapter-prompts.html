Foundations of Claude — Week 4

Plot Hole Check & Chapter Prompts

Lesson 16 — Phase 5. The hand-off to drafting.

How this works

Two prompts. First — Claude reviews your outline and flags plot holes, timeline issues, character inconsistencies. You decide how to fix each one. Claude re-outputs the corrected outline.

Second — Claude turns the corrected outline into per-chapter prompt artifacts. One prompt per chapter. Each one becomes the input for ONE drafting chat.

After this lesson, you draft. Claude is the writer. You are the editor.

STEP 1

Paste Your Outline

Drop your full outline here. Claude needs the complete document to run the 20-point check.

Plot Hole Check Prompt

Generate, copy, paste into a fresh Claude conversation in your project. Claude returns a prioritized list — CRITICAL, RECOMMENDED, OPTIONAL.

Click Generate to build your Plot Hole Check Prompt.
STEP 2

Review Claude's Findings

Claude returns a list of issues. You decide which are real problems, which are intentional choices it misread, and how to fix the real ones.

Don't move forward until: all CRITICAL issues are resolved, RECOMMENDED issues are resolved or consciously deferred, and the corrected outline is saved.

Once you've written your fix directive, paste it into the same Claude conversation and ask Claude to re-output the full corrected outline. Save the result as the corrected version.

STEP 3

Paste Your Corrected Outline

After Claude has fixed the plot holes, paste the corrected outline here. Then we generate the chapter prompts.
