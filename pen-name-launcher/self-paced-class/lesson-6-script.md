## LAUNCH TIMELINE: 4-6 WEEKS BEFORE LAUNCH

[SHOW ON SCREEN: Calendar showing pre-launch timeline]

You don't launch cold. You build anticipation.

**4-6 weeks before:**

**Assemble your ARC team.** ARC stands for Advanced Reader Copies. These are beta readers who get your finished book before launch, read it, and leave reviews.

[PAUSE]

Why this matters: Amazon's algorithm notices day-one reviews. A book with 50 reviews on launch day ranks higher than one with 5. Your ARC team is that first wave of legitimacy.

How to get an ARC team: Use Booksprout (free, or ~$10/month for unlimited). Post your book. Readers request copies. They read and review. You get 20-100+ launch day reviews depending on genre and how appealing your blurb is.

[PAUSE]

**Build your launch email list.** Email your newsletter (if you have one). Let people know the book is coming. If you're launching a reader magnet first (which we covered in Lesson 2), this is where those subscribers convert to readers.

**Pre-order setup (optional).** You can go live on KDP with a specific launch date. Readers can pre-order starting now. Pre-orders also count toward launch week sales for ranking purposes.

## AMAZON MARKETING SERVICES (AMS) ADS

[SHOW ON SCREEN: AMS dashboard showing campaign setup]

AMS is Amazon's self-serve advertising platform. Your book gets shown to Amazon shoppers searching relevant keywords and browsing similar books.

Here's how it works:

**Automatic Campaigns:**
Amazon chooses keywords and targets. You set a daily budget and a bid amount (how much you'll pay per click). Easy, but less control.

Recommended setup: Start with $5/day automatic. Let it run for 5 days. See what keywords convert.

[PAUSE]

**Manual Campaigns:**
You choose keywords. You set bids per keyword. More control, more work.

Recommended setup: After you see which keywords work in your automatic campaign, create a manual campaign targeting those keywords specifically.

[PAUSE]

**Budget and Bid Strategy:**

Start small. $5-10/day first week. Monitor your ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sale).

ACoS = (ad spend ÷ ad revenue) × 100

Example: You spend $100 on ads. Your ads generate $300 in book sales. ACoS = ($100 ÷ $300) × 100 = 33%.

At 33% ACoS on a $2.99 ebook earning $2.09 per sale, you're profitable.

[PAUSE]

Target ACoS: Under 50% is good for launch week. Under 40% is sustainable long-term.

**Realistic expectations:**
Most new books take 3-5 days to gain traction. Day 1-2 might have no sales. That's normal. Don't panic and increase your bid.

I usually run $7-10/day for the first 2 weeks of a new pen name launch. Then assess whether it's profitable to continue.

## BOOKBUB STRATEGY

[SHOW ON SCREEN: BookBub Featured Deals and BookBub Ads interface]

BookBub has two services: Featured Deals and BookBub Ads.

**BookBub Featured Deals:**
BookBub features your book on their email list (millions of subscribers). Your book gets a massive discount (usually $0.99 from $2.99+). You get thousands of sales in one day.

Cost: $100-$600 depending on your genre and release date. Romance is expensive. Literary fiction is cheaper.

Timeline: 2-4 weeks ahead of the launch you want featured.

[PAUSE]

**Should you do a Featured Deal at launch?**

Honestly: not always. Here's the split:

**Do a Featured Deal if:**
- You have a 3+ book series (readers binge-buy subsequent books at full price)
- You can afford it ($200+) and still be profitable at $0.99 price
- You're launching a hot backlist book (book 5 in a successful series)

**Skip the Featured Deal if:**
- This is your first book ever
- You can't afford the fee and break even
- You're testing whether your book will sell at all

[PAUSE]

I didn't do a BookBub Featured Deal for my first book. Too much risk. By book 3, I had data showing paranormal romance sold well, so I paid for a Featured Deal on book 5. Best $400 I spent. Made $5000 in sales in 72 hours.

[PAUSE]

**BookBub Ads:**
Different product. Similar to AMS ads. BookBub shows ads to their subscriber base when people browse categories. Less dramatic than Featured Deals, but steadier.

Cost: $5/day minimum, typically $5-15/day for launches.

I run BookBub Ads alongside AMS for maximum visibility.

## SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY: PICK ONE

[SHOW ON SCREEN: BookTok, Bookstagram, and Facebook icons]

Don't try all three platforms. Pick one you can sustain and do it well.

**BookTok (TikTok):**
- Audience: Gen Z and younger millennials, very engaged
- Format: 60-second videos (trending sounds, author intros, book recommendations, emotional clips)
- Viral potential: HIGH. A single video can break a book's ranking.
- Effort: 3-5 videos/week to stay relevant
- Best for: Any genre that has emotional resonance (paranormal, dark romance, emotional contemporary)

Example of BookTok success: Colleen Hoover's books went from moderate sales to blockbuster status because of BookTok.

[PAUSE]

**Bookstagram (Instagram):**
- Audience: Older readers (30-60s mostly), established book community
- Format: Beautiful photos of books, reviews, reading updates
- Viral potential: Moderate
- Effort: 3-5 posts/week with consistent aesthetic
- Best for: Romance, fantasy, any visually appealing genre

Instagram is slower burn than TikTok but more sustainable long-term. Readers here are committed book buyers.

[PAUSE]

**Facebook (Groups and Page):**
- Audience: Older readers, but very engaged
- Format: Group discussions, release announcements, reader engagement
- Viral potential: Low to moderate
- Effort: Daily engagement in groups, weekly page posts
- Best for: Building a direct community you own (vs algorithm-dependent)

Facebook isn't flashy but offers the most community control. Your Facebook group is yours. TikTok algorithm can kill your reach overnight.

[PAUSE]

**My honest take:**
If you're young and comfortable on video, do BookTok. If you're visual and patient, Bookstagram. If you're community-focused and want sustainability, Facebook. Don't do all three when you're launching.

## BUILDING YOUR ARC TEAM WITH BOOKSPROUT

[SHOW ON SCREEN: Booksprout dashboard and profile setup]

Booksprout (booksprout.co) connects authors with ARC readers.

**Setup takes 20 minutes:**
1. Create account
2. Add your book (title, description, genre, cover)
3. Set giveaway details (how many ARC copies available)
4. Publish

Readers see your book, request copies. You approve them (or it's automatic). They read. They review.

[PAUSE]

**Cost:**
- Free tier: Limited readers
- $10/month: Unlimited giveaways

Most authors use the free tier for their first book, then upgrade if they like the results.

**Realistic expectations:**
- 20-50 readers request copies at free tier
- 30-60% of those actually read and review
- You'll get 6-30 launch-day reviews

That's enough to move the algorithm needle on launch day.

## REALISTIC FIRST 30 DAYS

[SHOW ON SCREEN: Sales curve chart showing realistic expectations]

Here's what actually happens:

**Days 1-3 (Launch week):**
- ARC reviews roll in (20-50 reviews)
- Launch day sales spike (anywhere from 10 to 200+ copies depending on platform and list size)
- Ranking spikes (usually 10,000-100,000 in category depending on visibility)
- Visibility momentum builds

[PAUSE]

**Days 4-7:**
- Sales normalize (5-50 copies/day depending on advertising)
- Review velocity slows
- Ranking settles
- AMS ads perform (or don't) based on targeting

[PAUSE]

**Days 8-30:**
- Word-of-mouth sales trickle in
- Ranking stabilizes
- You stop actively promoting
- Ads continue if profitable

**Honest numbers:**
Most first-time indie authors see 50-200 copies sold in first 30 days if they do the marketing work. If they do nothing, 5-20.

Series authors who launch book 2 and 3 see higher numbers because their existing readers buy immediately.

## TOTAL COST SUMMARY: LAUNCH PHASE

[SHOW ON SCREEN: Cost comparison table]

**Minimum Launch** (just ads, no BookBub)
- Booksprout (free tier): $0
- AMS ads ($7/day × 14 days): $98
- BookBub Ads (optional, $5/day × 14 days): $70
- Total: ~$168 (if no BookBub Ads: ~$98)

**Recommended Launch** (AMS + BookBub Ads + some investment)
- Booksprout: $10 (one month)
- AMS ads ($10/day × 30 days): $300
- BookBub Ads ($7/day × 30 days): $210
- Optional: BookBub Featured Deal: $200
- Total: $720 (without Featured Deal: $520)

**Premium Launch** (Full marketing stack)
- Booksprout: $10/month
- AMS ads ($10/day × 30 days): $300
- BookBub Ads ($10/day × 30 days): $300
- BookBub Featured Deal (romance): $400
- Social media management or content creation: $200-500
- Total: $1210-1510

[PAUSE]

**Real talk:** You can launch successfully on $100 if you have a newsletter with 500+ subscribers. You can spend $1500 and still fail if your book isn't ready or your marketing targets the wrong audience.

The difference between $98 and $1210 isn't always worth it for book 1. Save the big spend for when you know your genre and audience.

## WHAT TO DO AFTER LAUNCH

[SHOW ON SCREEN: 30+ days timeline]

Launch week ends. The algorithm noise settles. Now what?

**Weeks 2-4:**
- Collect reviews and testimonials
- Engage with readers (respond to reviews, messages)
- Adjust AMS bids based on performance
- Continue social media (if you chose a platform)

**Weeks 4-8:**
- Start writing Book 2 (seriously)
- Monitor sales and ranking
- Decide if you're continuing ads (profitable or not?)
- Grow your newsletter
- Plan reader magnet for next book

**Month 2+:**
- Launch Book 2 (faster now because you've done it before)
- Build series momentum
- Reinvest launch profits into ads for Book 1 and 2
- Grow newsletter aggressively

[PAUSE]

The hard truth: a successful pen name isn't a one-book play. You need series momentum. Books 1-3 build trust. Books 4+ build sustainable income.

Most successful authors I know didn't see real money from a single pen name until they had 4+ books.
