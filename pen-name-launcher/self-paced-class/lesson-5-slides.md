### Slide 5: KINDLE UNLIMITED EXPLAINED
**Visual: Kindle Unlimited logo + explanation**
- Exclusive to Amazon (no Apple, Kobo, B&N, libraries)
- Readers pay for KU subscription
- Authors paid from KENP (Kindle Edition Normalized Pages) pool
- Payment based on pages read, not copies sold

---

### Slide 6: HOW KENP PAYMENT WORKS
**Example Calculation:**
- Monthly KENP fund: $50,000,000
- Total pages read all authors: 150,000,000
- Your book pages read: 150,000
- Your payment: (150,000 ÷ 150,000,000) × $50,000,000 = $50,000

Text: "Your earnings = (Your pages ÷ Total pages) × Monthly fund"

Key: Earnings vary monthly. Pool size changes $20-40M month to month.

### Slide 7: WIDE DISTRIBUTION EXPLAINED
**Visual: Distribution map showing multiple platforms**
- Available on: Apple Books, Kobo, B&N, Google Play, libraries, more
- Readers pay per copy
- You control pricing
- No exclusivity requirement

Text: "Your book wherever readers actually shop."

---

### Slide 8: KINDLE UNLIMITED PROS & CONS
**PROS:**
- Amazon algorithm boost for KU books
- Page read earnings can be substantial (especially series)
- Exclusive promotions available
- Readers more likely to finish KU books

**CONS:**
- Exclusive to Amazon only
- Earnings depend on monthly KENP pool (variable)
- 90-day minimum commitment
- Can't test other platforms during KU period

---

### Slide 9: WIDE DISTRIBUTION PROS & CONS
**PROS:**
- Available on every major retailer
- You control pricing ($0.99-$99.99)
- Can run free promotions for marketing
- Libraries stock wide books
- Better for existing audiences (newsletters, TikTok)

**CONS:**
- Amazon doesn't promote wide books as aggressively
- No KENP earnings
- Harder to run mass promotions
- Slower discoverability on Amazon

---

### Slide 10: KU VS WIDE DECISION FRAMEWORK
**START WITH KU IF:**
- First book in new pen name
- Planning a series (KU rewards binge readers)
- No existing audience outside Amazon
- Want to test KENP payouts

**GO WIDE IF:**
- Have established newsletter/social audience
- Using TikTok/BookstaTok to drive sales
- Want maximum distribution
- Have tested KU and it's not working

Text: "You can go wide AFTER your 90-day KU commitment. Try KU first."

### Slide 13: PRICING STRATEGY: THE MATH
**KDP Royalty Tiers:**
- $0.99-2.98: 35% royalty
- **$2.99-9.99: 70% royalty** ← SWEET SPOT
- $10+: 35% royalty

**The Numbers:**
- $0.99 book: earn $0.35 per sale (higher volume, lower per-sale revenue)
- **$2.99 book: earn $2.09 per sale** (balance of volume + revenue)
- $4.99 book: earn $3.49 per sale (lower volume)

Text: "$2.99 is the magic price point for indie romance."

---

### Slide 14: PRICING LAUNCH STRATEGY
**Option A: Launch at $2.99**
- Steady, no strategy needed
- Builds ranking organically

**Option B: Launch at $0.99, then raise**
- Week 1: $0.99 (visibility boost)
- Week 2+: $2.99 (permanent)
- Monthly $0.99 promotions to refresh ranking

**Option C: Variable by season**
- Launch promotions: $0.99
- Regular price: $2.99
- Seasonal boosts: $0.99

Text: "Most successful authors use Option B or C."

### Slide 15: PAPERBACK & HARDCOVER PRICING
**Paperback:**
- Set price covering production ($6-8 for 300-page) + royalty
- KDP calculates minimum. Don't go below it.
- Typical: $12.99-17.99

**Hardcover:**
- Premium pricing: $17.99-24.99
- Fewer sales but higher per-copy royalty
- Add after ebook is selling

Text: "Hardcover is optional and comes later."
