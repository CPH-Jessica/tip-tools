### Slide 3: 4-6 WEEKS BEFORE: ARC TEAM
**What is an ARC?**
- Advanced Reader Copy
- Free copy for early readers in exchange for reviews
- 20-50+ launch day reviews from ARC readers
- Signals legitimacy to Amazon's algorithm

**Why it matters:**
- Books with 50 day-one reviews rank higher than those with 5
- Day-one review velocity is ranked by Amazon's algorithm
- Your ARC team is your first wave of legitimacy

---

### Slide 4: BOOKSPROUT ARC SETUP
**Process:**
1. Create account at booksprout.co
2. Add book (title, description, genre, cover)
3. Set number of ARC copies available
4. Publish
5. Readers request. You approve (or auto-approve). They read. They review.

**Cost:**
- Free: Limited readers
- $10/month: Unlimited

**Timeline:** Setup takes 20 minutes. Run for 4-6 weeks before launch.

### Slide 8: AMAZON MARKETING SERVICES (AMS)
**What AMS Does:**
- Shows your book to Amazon shoppers
- Appears in search results for keywords you target
- Appears on related product pages
- Readers click → go to your book → buy (hopefully)

**Cost Model:**
- Pay per click (not per sale)
- You set daily budget ($5-50/day typical)
- You set bid amount (how much per click)
- You pay only when someone clicks

---

### Slide 9: AMS CAMPAIGN TYPES
**Automatic Campaigns:**
- Amazon chooses keywords and targets
- Low effort, less control
- Recommended: Start here for 5 days, see what works

**Manual Campaigns:**
- You choose keywords
- You set bids per keyword
- More control, more work
- Recommended: Launch after seeing automatic campaign results

---

### Slide 10: AMS BUDGET & BID STRATEGY
**Starting Point:** $5-10/day first week

**Key Metric: ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sale)**
- ACoS = (ad spend ÷ ad revenue) × 100
- Example: Spend $100, generate $300 in sales = 33% ACoS
- Target: Under 50% for launch (under 40% sustainable)

**Realistic Expectations:**
- Days 1-2 might have zero sales (normal)
- Takes 3-5 days to gain traction
- Don't panic and increase bid early

Text: "$7-10/day for first 2 weeks is standard."

---

### Slide 11: BOOKBUB FEATURED DEALS EXPLAINED
**What it is:**
- BookBub (millions of subscribers) features your book
- Usually at deep discount ($0.99 from $2.99+)
- Thousands of sales in 24-72 hours

**Cost:** $100-$600 depending on genre and date

**Timeline:** Book 2-4 weeks ahead of desired feature date

**ROI Example:**
- Cost: $400
- Sales: 5000 copies at $0.99
- Gross: $5000
- Net: $4600 profit + visibility boost

---

### Slide 12: WHEN TO DO BOOKBUB FEATURED DEALS
**DO a Featured Deal If:**
- You have a 3+ book series (readers binge buy sequels)
- You can afford it and break even at $0.99
- You're launching book 5+ in successful series

**SKIP Featured Deals If:**
- This is your first book ever
- You can't afford it
- You don't know if your book will sell

Text: "Jessica skipped it for book 1, invested in book 5 when she had data."

---

### Slide 13: BOOKBUB ADS VS FEATURED DEALS
| Feature | Featured Deal | BookBub Ads |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| Cost | $100-600 | $5-15/day |
| Impact | Massive spike | Steady sales |
| Duration | 1-3 days | Ongoing |
| Best for | Series | Testing/sustained |

Text: "Use both if you can. BookBub Ads alone is worth it."

### Slide 14: SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY: PICK ONE
**BookTok (TikTok):**
- Audience: Gen Z/younger millennials
- Format: 60-second emotional/trending videos
- Viral potential: HIGH
- Effort: 3-5 videos/week
- Colleen Hoover effect: Possible

**Bookstagram (Instagram):**
- Audience: 30-60, established book community
- Format: Beautiful photos, reviews, reading updates
- Viral potential: Moderate
- Effort: 3-5 posts/week
- Sustainability: Very high

**Facebook:**
- Audience: Older readers
- Format: Group discussions, announcements
- Community: Most owned/controlled
- Effort: Daily engagement
- Sustainability: Highest

Text: "One platform done well > three platforms done badly."

### Slide 17: REALISTIC FIRST 30 DAYS
**Days 1-3 (Launch week):**
- ARC reviews: 20-50
- Sales: 10-200+ (depends on list size and visibility)
- Ranking spike: 10,000-100,000 in category

**Days 4-7:**
- Sales normalize: 5-50/day
- Review velocity slows
- Ranking settles

**Days 8-30:**
- Word-of-mouth sales trickle in
- Ranking stabilizes
- Ads continue if profitable

Text: "Realistic: 50-200 copies in first 30 days with marketing effort."
