## SLIDE 3: What IS a Reader Magnet?
**Title:** "Reader Magnet Definition"
**Content:**
- A complete, standalone story
- Same voice & style as your books
- Shorter than your novels (15,000-25,000 words)
- Free in exchange for an email
- Goal: prove your voice, get readers hooked

**It's NOT:** A sample chapter from your book

**Visual:** Gift box with story/book inside
**Style:** Clear, definitional

---

## SLIDE 4: Why You Need One
**Title:** "Reader Magnet = Launch Multiplier"
**Content:**
- Grows your newsletter (Email 3 welcome sequence)
- Proves your voice to new readers
- Turns subscribers into book readers
- Drives first-book sales on launch day
- Works 24/7 for you (forever)

**Visual:** Multiplier icon or growth curve
**Style:** Strategic value focused

## SLIDE 5: Format Options
**Title:** "Choose Your Format"
**Content:**

| Format | Word Count | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-----------|------|------|
| **Short Story** | 5-10K | Quick to write | Too short for voice showcase |
| **Novella** | 15-25K | Perfect length ✓ | Requires focus & time |
| **Prequel** | 20-30K | Characters readers know | Must write book first |

**Recommendation: Novella (15-25K)**

**Visual:** 3-format comparison table
**Style:** Data-driven, clear recommendation

---

## SLIDE 6: Why Novella Length
**Title:** "Why 15,000-25,000 Words Is Perfect"
**Content:**
- Long enough: Emotional depth + multiple scenes + real stakes
- Short enough: Readers finish in 2-3 hours
- Reader magnet effect: Long enough to love your voice
- Writing timeline: Achievable in 3 weeks
- Publishing timeline: Ready before book launch

**Visual:** Goldilocks zone on a spectrum (too short — just right — too long)
**Style:** Visual balance emphasizer

---

## SLIDE 7: The 5 Core Elements
**Title:** "Every Reader Magnet Needs These 5"
**Content:**
1. **Protagonist** — character with depth, flaws, desires
2. **Signature Tropes** — what you're known for
3. **Heat Level** — matching your books
4. **Emotional Arc** — transformation from Point A to Point B
5. **Reason to Want More** — readers buy your books after

**Visual:** 5-element diagram or circular flow
**Style:** Foundational, clear

---

## SLIDE 8: Element 1 — Protagonist
**Title:** "Protagonist: A Character Readers Care About"
**Content:**
- Not a stereotype; a real person
- With depth, backstory, wounds
- With desires (what they want)
- With fears (what they're afraid of)
- Hero + heroine (if romance) both need arcs

**Visual:** Character profile card template
**Style:** Character-focused

---

## SLIDE 9: Element 2 — Signature Tropes
**Title:** "Use Your Signature Tropes"
**Content:**
- Remember: Forced proximity? Grumpy/sunshine? Found family?
- Use them here
- Readers finish magnet and think: "Oh! THIS is what she writes!"
- Consistency = recognition = brand loyalty

**Visual:** Trope icons or badges showing 2-3 tropes in action
**Style:** Brand-building focused

---

## SLIDE 10: Element 3 — Heat Level Alignment
**Title:** "Match Your Book Heat Level"
**Content:**
- Reader magnet heat level = Book heat level
- If magnet is closed door but book is spicy: reader disappointment
- If magnet is spicy but book is warm: reader confusion

**Rule:** What you show in the magnet is what they expect in the book

**Visual:** Matching pair, or arrow showing alignment
**Style:** Expectation-setting, clear rule

---

## SLIDE 11: Element 4 — Emotional Arc
**Title:** "The Heart: Emotional Transformation"
**Content:**
- Point A: Where character starts (broken, afraid, guarded, alone)
- The journey: What happens, challenges faced, growth moments
- Point B: Where character ends (healed, loved, whole, accepted)

**Example:** Guarded loner → Learns to trust → Opens her heart

**Visual:** Journey diagram showing A → journey → B
**Style:** Transformational, emotional

---

## SLIDE 12: Element 5 — Reason to Want More
**Title:** "Why Readers Will Buy Your Books"
**Content:**
- Reader magnet ends (not cliffhanger, but complete story)
- Reader finishes and thinks: "I want to live in this world forever"
- Reader immediately clicks Amazon to buy your book
- This is not automatic. It requires emotional investment.

**Visual:** Heart or purchase icon, forward arrow
**Style:** Action-oriented, result-focused

---

## SLIDE 13: Story Bible Essentials
**Title:** "Before You Write: Create Your Story Bible"
**Content:**
- **Character profiles:** Hero, heroine, supporting cast
- **World-building:** Paranormal/fantasy/location specific rules
- **Plot outline:** Three acts, inciting incident, midpoint, climax
- **Tone & voice:** How you write, narrative perspective
- **Trope notes:** Which tropes, how they show up

**Time investment:** 1 hour, 3-5 pages

**Visual:** Story bible pages or document icon
**Style:** Planning/preparation focused

---

## SLIDE 14: Scene-by-Scene Outline
**Title:** "Outline: 10-15 Scenes"
**Content:**
- Scene 1: Inciting incident
- Scene 2-3: Rising conflict
- Scene 4-6: Emotional deepening
- Scene 7-9: Climax approaches
- Scene 10-15: Climax & resolution

**Each scene:** What happens, which POV, approximate words

**Time investment:** 30 minutes of focused planning

**Visual:** Scene list or outline template

## SLIDE 17: Editing Pass 1 — Quality Check
**Title:** "Edit Pass 1: Big Picture (Does It Work?)"
**Content:**
- Does the story make sense?
- Are there plot holes?
- Is pacing right (not too slow, not rushed)?
- Is emotional arc complete?
- All 5 Core Elements present and strong?
- Does ending satisfy and create want for more?

**Not:** Fixing grammar or sentences
**Is:** Fixing structure, logic, depth

**Visual:** Magnifying glass over big picture/structure elements
**Style:** Structural focus

---

## SLIDE 18: Editing Pass 2 — Line Edit
**Title:** "Edit Pass 2: Sentence Level (How It Reads)"
**Content:**
- Clarity: Do sentences make sense?
- Rhythm: Does prose flow naturally?
- Show vs. tell: Are you showing not explaining?
- Repetition: Any repeated words/phrases?
- Voice: Does this sound like YOU?

**Read:** On screen, looking for prose quality
**Listen:** For your voice, your rhythm

**Visual:** Individual sentence blocks, pencil for marking
**Style:** Craft-focused

---

## SLIDE 19: Editing Pass 3 — Dialogue Polish
**Title:** "Edit Pass 3: Dialogue (The Ear Test)"
**Content:**
- Read dialogue OUT LOUD
- Does it sound natural?
- Can you tell who's speaking without tags?
- Does each character have distinct voice?
- Any awkward phrasing or over-explaining?

**This pass is dialogue only. Everything else stays as is.**

**Visual:** Speech bubbles, ear icon
**Style:** Practical, audio-focused

---

## SLIDE 20: Professional Editing Decision
**Title:** "Do You Need a Professional Editor?"
**Content:**

**For reader magnet:**
- Optional, not required
- You can self-edit and publish
- Benefit of self-editing: your voice stays pure

**If you choose to hire:**
- Cost: $300-800 for novella
- Time: 2-4 weeks turnaround
- Value: Professional polish (optional for magnet)

**For your first book:** Much more valuable investment

**Visual:** Optional checkmark vs. checkmark
**Style:** Cost-conscious, honest
