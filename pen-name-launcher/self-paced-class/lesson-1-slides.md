# Lesson 1: Building Your Pen Name Identity
## Slide Descriptions (15-20 slides)

## SLIDE 2: The Problem
**Title:** "Why Most Authors Get This Wrong"
**Content:**
- No Amazon check = name conflicts
- No genre clarity = confused readers
- No heat level decision = mismatched expectations
- No target reader = trying to please everyone

**Visual:** Simple 4-panel graphic showing each mistake with an X over it
**Style:** Clean, direct, no fluff

---

## SLIDE 3: The Real Story
**Title:** "My First Pen Name Launch"
**Content:**
- Picked a name I liked
- Didn't check Amazon
- Found out 2 months in: someone already using it
- Months of confusion and fixing

**Visual:** Timeline with a big OOPS moment, or a before/after comparison
**Style:** Personal, vulnerable, real

---

## SLIDE 4: Step 1 — Your Pen Name
**Title:** "Choosing Your Pen Name"
**Content:**
- Names you actually like (you're saying this forever)
- Check Amazon (exact search)
- Check Goodreads (exact search)
- Consider discoverability (not too common, not too strange)
- Think about genre fit

**Visual:** Checklist or step-by-step numbered list
**Style:** Clear, actionable

---

## SLIDE 5: Name Examples
**Title:** "Genre Fit in Action"
**Content:**
- ✓ "Chase Sterling" = romantic suspense, military romance
- ✓ "Meadow Grace" = paranormal romance, contemporary romance
- ✗ "Chase Sterling" for cozy mystery
- ✗ "Meadow Grace" for dark fantasy

**Visual:** Two columns (right fit / wrong fit) with checkmarks and X marks
**Style:** Simple visual comparison

---

## SLIDE 6: The Heat Level Scale
**Title:** "Understanding Heat Levels"
**Content:** A visual scale (1-5) showing:
- 1: Sweet/Closed Door (no sex on page)
- 2: Warm (fade to black)
- 3: Hot (explicit, multiple scenes)
- 4: Very Hot (frequent, integral)
- 5: Extreme (specific kinks, very targeted)

**Visual:** Thermometer style or 1-5 scale with temperature imagery
**Style:** Color-coded (cool to hot)

---

## SLIDE 7: Heat Level Reader Expectations
**Title:** "What Each Heat Level Delivers"
**Content:**
- Heat 1: Hallmark movies, inspirational, cozy
- Heat 2: Mainstream contemporary romance
- Heat 3: Most category romance, self-published romance
- Heat 4: Erotic romance, paranormal romance
- Heat 5: BDSM, specific communities

**Visual:** Reader types icons or simple category labels
**Style:** Educational, neutral tone

---

## SLIDE 8: The Danger of Misalignment
**Title:** "Heat Level Mismatch = Lost Readers"
**Content:**
- Writing Heat 5 paranormal romance
- But paranormal readers expect Heat 1-2
- Book disappoints both audiences
- Know your choice, serve your reader

**Visual:** X icon over mismatched book + reader expectation
**Style:** Warning/awareness focused

---

## SLIDE 10: Why Tropes Matter
**Title:** "Tropes Build Your Author Brand"
**Content:**

- Consistent tropes = consistent readers
- Your genuine preferences = sustainable career

**Visual:** Cycle arrow showing: Book 1 → Reader Promise → Book 2 → Reader returns
**Style:** Relationship-focused

## SLIDE 11: Target Reader Profile
**Title:** "Define Your Reader (Be Specific)"
**Content:**
- Age range (rough)
- Life stage
- What she's looking for (emotional core)
- Heat level preference
- What irritates her
- Why she reads romance

**Visual:** Audience persona template or simple profile card
**Style:** Practical worksheet feel

---

## SLIDE 12: Target Reader Example
**Title:** "My Paranormal Romance Reader"
**Content:**
- Loves paranormal worlds (vampires, shifters, witches)
- Wants emotional depth + slow-burn tension
- Values found family and loyalty
- Reads at night; wants to stay up late
- Frustrated by insta-love and weak conflicts
- Heat Level 3

**Visual:** Single reader profile card with these details
**Style:** Real example, detailed

---

## SLIDE 13: Author Voice
**Title:** "Your Author Voice (The Way You Write)"
**Content:**
- Witty and sarcastic? Own it.
- Lyrical and descriptive? Lean in.
- Sparse and direct? That's your strength.
- Funny? Intense? Romantic? Dreamy?

**Voice is not something you develop—it's something you recognize and amplify.**

**Visual:** Text variations showing different voices (sarcastic vs. lyrical snippets)
**Style:** Writing sample focused

## SLIDE 14: Visual Brand Identity
**Title:** "Building Your Brand Identity"
**Content:**
- Color palette (2-3 primary colors)
- Primary font (logo, pen name)
- Secondary font (body, subtitles)
- Visual mark or logo (simple is okay)
- Consistency across covers, newsletter, website

**Visual:** Example color palettes shown side-by-side for different genres
**Style:** Visual/design focused

---

## SLIDE 15: Genre-Specific Brand Examples
**Title:** "Brand Identity By Genre"
**Content:**

**Dark Romance:** Deep jewel tones, black, rich red, serif fonts, moody imagery
**Contemporary Romance:** Blush, cream, sage, sans-serif, fresh imagery
**Paranormal Romance:** Midnight blue, silver, deep purple, atmospheric fonts

**Visual:** Three brand identity boards side-by-side showing color + fonts + style

## SLIDE 16: Author Bio (Three Lengths)
**Title:** "You Need Three Author Bios"
**Content:**
- **Short (30-50 words):** Book covers, Amazon profile, descriptions
- **Medium (75-125 words):** Newsletter footer, website, collaborations
- **Long (150-250 words):** Press kits, interviews, speaking bios

**Key:** Make all three authentic and use real details from your life.

**Visual:** Three boxes with word counts and examples
**Style:** Structural/formatting focused

---

**Title:** "Real Bio Examples (Medium Length)"

A former librarian, she spent her twenties reading everything and her thirties writing the paranormal romances she couldn't put down. When she's not writing, she's probably in a bookstore or planning her next trip to the Oregon coast. She lives in Portland with her partner, two rescue cats, and too many plants."

## SLIDE 18: Your Tagline
**Title:** "Create Your Author Tagline"
**Content:**
- Short phrase capturing your author brand
- Not your pen name, but your promise
- Examples:
  - "Paranormal romance with bite and heart"
  - "Grumpy heroes, found family, guaranteed happy endings"
  - "Dark, emotional, unforgettable"
  - "Slow-burn paranormal romance"

**Visual:** Tagline examples in varied typography
**Style:** Inspirational, design-forward

---

## SLIDE 19: Tagline Qualities
**Title:** "Your Tagline Should Be..."

- **Typography:** Consistent hierarchy; headlines should be large and clear
- **Imagery:** Professional but warm (not stock-photo fake)
- **Layout:** Plenty of white space; never text-heavy
- **Tone:** Educational, not salesy; direct, not fluffy
- **Consistency:** Same design elements across all 20+ slides
