## LESSON 5: PUBLISHING ON KDP AND GOING WIDE
**Phases 7-8 Covered**

### Script: `lesson-5-script.md`
- What KDP offers (ebook, paperback, hardcover)
- KDP account setup (15 minutes)
- **THE BIG QUESTION — Kindle Unlimited decision**
  - What KU is (exclusivity, KENP payment model)
  - How KENP pays (percentage of monthly pool based on pages read)
  - KU pros/cons (algorithm boost vs limited distribution)
  - Wide distribution pros/cons (control vs discoverability)
  - Honest decision framework (when to choose each)
- Categories and keywords (Publisher Rocket $199 vs manual method)
- Pricing strategy (the $2.99 sweet spot for 70% royalty)
- Launch price strategies (immediate vs $0.99 temporary)
- Formatting options (Vellum $249.99, Atticus $147, Kindle Create free, D2D free)
- Draft2Digital and wide distribution (what it is, where it distributes, 10% commission)
- Real costs for everything
- Complete publishing workflow
- 2-week timeline from finished manuscript to live book

**Key Teaching Points:**
- "The KU decision kept me up at night on my first pen name"
- Try KU first if you're new (lock in 90 days, then assess)
- $2.99 is the magic price point (70% royalty sweet spot)
- Formatting tools are one-time investments ($249.99 for Vellum pays for itself)
- Wide distribution is harder on Amazon's algorithm but maximizes reach

## LESSON 6: LAUNCH WEEK AND BEYOND
**Phases 9-10 Covered**

### Script: `lesson-6-script.md`
- 6-8 week launch timeline
- 4-6 weeks before (ARC team with Booksprout, email list, pre-orders)
- Launch week (AMS ads, social media, engagement)
- **AMS (Amazon Marketing Services) ads basics**
  - Automatic vs manual campaigns
  - Budget and bid strategy ($5-10/day starting)
  - ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sale) metric
  - Realistic expectations (3-5 days to traction)
- **BookBub strategy**
  - Featured Deals vs BookBub Ads
  - Cost and ROI
  - When to book (when to skip)
  - Jessica's example: Skipped book 1, paid for book 5
- **Social media platform choice (pick ONE)**
  - BookTok (viral potential, 3-5 videos/week)
  - Bookstagram (sustainable, 30-60 audience, visual aesthetic)
  - Facebook (owned community, daily engagement)
- Building review team with Booksprout (free or ~$10/month)
- Realistic first 30 days (50-200 copies with marketing)
- What to do after launch (write book 2, grow newsletter, reinvest)
- Total cost summary (min $22.50/mo, recommended $195-345/mo, premium $505+/mo)
- **Strong CTA closing:** "You now know more about launching a pen name than most authors with years of experience. Pen Name Launcher plugin + The Invisible Pen Skool community."

**Key Teaching Points:**
- "Everything comes down to launch week"
- ARC team = day-one review legitimacy
- AMS is affordable and scalable ($7-10/day is standard)
- BookBub is optional for book 1 but powerful for book 5+
- Pick ONE social platform and do it well
- Book 1 doesn't need to be perfect — book 2 will be better because you did this once
- Most successful authors have a series (need 3-4 books for sustainable income)

## TEACHING VOICE & TONE

All content follows Jessica's author education voice:

- **Direct and practical** — "Here's exactly what I did when I launched my 8th pen name"
- **Earned authority** — Not theory, real experience from multiple pen name launches
- **No corporate jargon** — No "leveraging" or "synergies," just honest talk
- **No toxic positivity** — Realistic about what works and what doesn't
- **Short paragraphs** — Easy to follow, doesn't overwhelm
- **[PAUSE] and [SHOW ON SCREEN] markers** — Avatar script formatting for natural video pacing
- **Real numbers** — Actual costs, actual timelines, actual sales expectations
- **Honest trade-offs** — Why you might skip BookBub for book 1, when DIY makes sense vs when to outsource

## HOW TO USE THIS CONTENT

1. Read the script to understand messaging and pacing
2. Use slide descriptions to create visual slides in your preferred tool (Figma, Canva, PowerPoint)
3. Record avatar video with script (use AI avatar tool of choice)

1. Watch the avatar video (15-20 minutes per lesson)
2. Follow along with slide presentation
3. Complete the worksheet (working documents, not graded)
4. Reference checklists as you do the actual work
