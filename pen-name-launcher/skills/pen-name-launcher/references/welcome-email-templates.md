# Welcome Email Templates for New Pen Name Launch

Use these three emails to introduce your new pen name to readers who've subscribed for your reader magnet. Customize with your [PLACEHOLDER] details and send 1-2 times per week.

---

## Email 1: Welcome + Reader Magnet Delivery

**Subject line options:**
- Your [READER MAGNET TITLE] is ready
- Welcome to the [PEN NAME] newsletter
- Here's what you signed up for

**Body (150-200 words):**

Hi [READER FIRST NAME],

Welcome! You're about to read [READER MAGNET TITLE], and I'm thrilled you're here.

I'm [PEN NAME], and I write [GENRE: e.g., steamy contemporary romance/paranormal fantasy]. This newsletter is my direct line to readers like you—no algorithm gatekeeping, just me sharing book updates, writing process peeks, and occasional personal rambles about why I can't stop writing about [TROPE: e.g., grumpy heroes/found family].

Your reader magnet is attached. No catch, no signup funnel. Download it, read it, enjoy it. If you love it, you'll love the full [SERIES NAME] series coming later this year.

Want to know what's next? Stick around. I send about one email per week, and I promise never to spam you or sell your email to anyone.

Here's to the start of something good.

[PEN NAME]

P.S. Reply to this email anytime. I read everything.

## Email 2: Personal Connection + Book Recommendations

**Subject line options:**
- Why I wrote this character
- The book that inspired [YOUR BOOK TITLE]
- [PEN NAME]'s guilty pleasure reading list

**Body (180-220 words):**

Hi [READER FIRST NAME],

I want to tell you why I wrote [YOUR BOOK TITLE]—because the story matters more than the sales pitch.

I wrote this book because I couldn't stop thinking about [REASON: e.g., what it would feel like to fall in love with someone you're supposed to hate / a world where magic has a price nobody talks about]. The main character, [CHARACTER NAME], grew out of my frustration with [CHARACTER MOTIVATION]. I wanted to write a story where [CORE CONFLICT THEME] wasn't solved in 10 minutes—where it actually cost something.

If you loved [READER MAGNET TITLE], you'll probably love these books too:
- [COMPARABLE TITLE] by [AUTHOR]
- [COMPARABLE TITLE] by [AUTHOR]
- [COMPARABLE TITLE] by [AUTHOR]

(These are genuine recommendations, not affiliate links. I just think they're excellent and want you to find books you love.)

The first book in the [SERIES NAME] series drops [RELEASE DATE]. Pre-orders go live [DATE]. I'll send you the link as soon as it's live.

What character from [READER MAGNET TITLE] do you want to see get their own book? Hit reply—I'm collecting ideas.

[PEN NAME]

## Email 3: What's Coming Next + Community Invite

**Subject line options:**
- You're invited: join the [PEN NAME] community

- One more thing before [BOOK TITLE] drops

[BOOK TITLE] releases [RELEASE DATE], and I'm giving you a heads-up before everyone else sees it.

Pre-orders go live [DATE] at [RETAILER LINKS]. If you grab an early copy, you'll be the first to know which character gets book two, because the twist is hiding in the epilogue. No spoilers. Just early reader privilege.

Beyond this one book, here's what's coming:
- [BOOK TITLE 2], [RELEASE DATE]
- [BOOK TITLE 3], [RELEASE DATE]
- A [GENRE] spin-off series starting [RELEASE DATE]

And here's the personal ask: I'm building a community of readers who love [GENRE/TROPE] as much as I do. We have a [PLATFORM: Discord/Facebook Group/Slack] where people share:

[JOIN LINK]

No pressure, no MLM vibes. Just readers talking about books, and me popping in to answer questions and geek out about tropes.

Either way, thank you for being here. You're the reason I get to keep writing.

[PEN NAME]

P.S. What trope do you want to see next? Reply and vote.

## Implementation Notes

**Send cadence:** Space these three emails 5-7 days apart. Your welcome sequence ends after email 3; move readers to your regular newsletter schedule after that.

**Personalization:** The [PLACEHOLDER] fields are non-negotiable. A generic email reads like spam. Spend 10 minutes filling these in before sending.

**Links:** Include affiliate links for book recommendations if you have them, but be honest about it. Readers appreciate transparency more than fake objectivity.

**Tone:** These templates use casual, conversational language because you're building a relationship, not broadcasting. Read each draft out loud before sending. If you wouldn't say it to a reader at a book event, rewrite it.
