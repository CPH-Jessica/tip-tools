# AMS Ads Quickstart: First-Time Setup Guide

Amazon Marketing Services (AMS) is your direct line to readers searching for books in your genre. This guide walks you through creating your first Sponsored Products campaign step-by-step, reading results, and making adjustments.

---

## What You're Doing (The 30-Second Version)

You're creating ads for your book that appear when readers search on Amazon for keywords related to your genre or tropes. You set a daily budget, bid on keywords, write ad copy, and Amazon shows your book to relevant readers. You only pay when someone clicks your ad.

**Why this matters:** AMS ads put your book in front of readers actively searching for what you write. It's not guessing; it's showing up where demand exists.

---

## Setting Up Your First Campaign

Log into Amazon Author Central (author.amazon.com). Navigate to Advertising > Sponsored Products.

**Campaign Details**

Click "Create Campaign." You'll fill in:

**Campaign Name:** Name this something you'll recognize later. "Genre-Name_Launch_April2026" works. You might run multiple campaigns; clear naming helps you track which is which.

**Targeting Type:** Choose "Automatic" for your first campaign. Automatic targeting lets Amazon match your book to relevant keywords based on your book's metadata. You'll bid on specific keywords later; automatic targeting is easier for beginners.

**Daily Budget:** Start with $5-10/day. This is not aggressive spending. You're testing. $10/day over 30 days = $300/month maximum spend. You can pause the campaign anytime.

**Bidding Strategy:** Select "Dynamic bids - down only." This means Amazon can lower your bid if the click seems less likely to convert, but won't raise it without your approval. Lower risk for beginners.

**Start Date & Duration:** Set your campaign to start immediately. For duration, select "No end date" so you can pause whenever you want.

Click "Save and Continue."

---

## Targeting and Keywords (Automatic Campaign)

On the next screen, you'll see "Automatic Targeting" already selected. Amazon defaults to showing your book when readers search for:
- Keywords related to your book's metadata (title, subtitle, description, categories)
- Keywords related to books you've listed as competitors
- Searches for books in your category

This is good for starting. You'll optimize keywords later.

If you want to manually add keywords now, you can, but leave that for campaign two. Your first campaign is learning.

---

## Ad Copy (The Ad Itself)

Your ad shows on Amazon search results. It includes:

**Product:** Your book (pre-selected).

**Headline (maximum 50 characters):** A short, compelling statement about your book. Examples:
- "Paranormal Romance: Fated Mates"
- "Dark & Steamy Love Story"
- "Enemies to Lovers: Paranormal"

This is smaller text; readers glance at it. Make it clear what you're offering.

**Ad Copy (maximum 250 characters):** A short description. Examples:
- "She never wanted a mate. He couldn't resist her. Their forbidden love ignites everything."
- "Trapped together. Enemies. Then everything changes. A paranormal paranormal romance."
- "When a ghost walks into her bookshop, she doesn't expect to fall in love."

Write conversational copy. Highlight your primary trope or hook.

---

## Launch Your Campaign

Review everything. Campaign name, budget, targeting, ad copy. Click "Launch Campaign."

Amazon will review your ad (usually within 24 hours). Once approved, your ads start running.

---

## Reading Your Results: Key Metrics Explained

Your campaign dashboard shows several metrics. Here's what they mean in plain English.

**Impressions:** The number of times your ad was shown to a reader. If you got 500 impressions, your ad appeared 500 times in search results.

Example: 500 impressions means 500 readers saw your book as an option.

**Clicks:** The number of times a reader clicked your ad. This is the next step—they saw the ad and clicked to view your book on Amazon.

Example: Out of 500 impressions, 15 clicks means 3% of people who saw your ad clicked it.

**Click-Through Rate (CTR):** Clicks divided by impressions, shown as a percentage. 
CTR = (Clicks / Impressions) × 100

Example: 15 clicks / 500 impressions = 3% CTR. 
A 3% CTR is solid for paranormal romance. Typical ranges are 0.5%-3%.

**Cost Per Click (CPC):** How much you paid per click. If you spent $10 and got 15 clicks, your CPC is $0.67.

Example: $10 spent / 15 clicks = $0.67 per click.

**Spend:** Total amount of money you've spent on the campaign. This comes from your daily budget.

Example: If your daily budget is $5 and you run for 5 days, max spend is $25 (unless you adjust mid-campaign).

**Advertising Cost of Sale (ACoS):** This is the big one. ACoS tells you whether your ads are profitable.

ACoS = (Ad Spend / Revenue from Ad Clicks) × 100

Example: You spent $100 on ads and made $400 in royalties from books sold via those ads. ACoS = ($100 / $400) × 100 = 25%.

A 25% ACoS means for every dollar you made, you spent $0.25 on ads. That's healthy. Aim for ACoS under 30% as a beginner. Anything under 50% is breakeven or better, depending on your royalty rate.

**Conversions:** The number of books sold (or pages read) that came from clicks on your ad.

Example: 15 clicks led to 3 book sales. Your conversion rate is 20% (3/15 = 20%). This is solid. Expect 10%-30% conversion depending on your book, price, and reviews.

## Interpreting Your First Week

After a week of running your first campaign, check your dashboard. You might see:

**Lots of impressions, few clicks:** Your ad is showing up, but the headline/copy isn't compelling. Revise your ad copy. Make it clearer what your book offers.

**Decent clicks, no conversions:** Readers like your ad enough to click, but your book page isn't converting them. Check: Is your description clear? Do you have reviews? Is your price competitive? Is your cover clickable? Fix the book page, not the ad.

**High ACoS (over 50%):** You're spending more on ads than you're making. Options: Lower your daily budget, adjust your bid, refine your targeting, or pause the campaign. This is normal for first campaigns. You're learning.

**Low impressions:** Your bid might be too low, or your keywords aren't matching reader searches. If you moved to manual keywords, consider lowering your bid to get more data, or broaden your keywords.

---

## Optimization Moves (Week 2+)

After a week, you have data. Use it.

**Pause underperforming keywords:** If you're using manual targeting and certain keywords have high spend and zero conversions, pause them. Don't throw money at dead keywords.

**Increase bid on winners:** If certain keywords are converting well with low ACoS, increase your bid slightly to get more visibility.

**Test new ad copy:** After a week, pause your original ad copy and create a variation. Try a different headline or angle. Run both and compare. Keep the winner.

**Adjust daily budget:** If you're consistently hitting your daily budget and results look good, increase it slightly. If you're seeing high spend with low conversions, lower it.

**Expand to manual targeting:** Once you understand your campaign, create a second campaign with manual keyword targeting. You'll bid on specific keywords you research (paranormal romance keywords, your trope names, comparable author names, etc.).

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Setting budget too high from the start:** Don't launch with a $50/day budget if you don't know what converts. Start low ($5-10), learn, then scale up.

**Not checking your book page:** If clicks aren't converting, the problem is usually not the ad—it's the book page. Are your description and cover selling the book? Do you have reviews?

**Expecting immediate profit:** AMS is marketing spend. You're building visibility. Your first campaign might break even or lose money. That's okay. You're buying data and learning what works.

**Ignoring low performers:** If a keyword or ad copy is tanking, pause it. Don't wait a month hoping it improves.

**Setting and forgetting:** Check your campaign weekly for the first month. After that, bi-weekly is fine. Algorithms change; you need to stay responsive.

---

## Your First Campaign Timeline

**Days 1-3:** Campaign launches, Amazon reviews and approves, ads start running.

**Days 3-7:** Monitor daily. You might see very few impressions (this is normal; Amazon is testing). Note which keywords are getting clicks.

**Day 7:** Review your data. Pause underperforming keywords if doing manual targeting. Adjust bid or daily budget if needed.

**Days 8-14:** Continue running. You should have enough data to see patterns.

**Day 14:** Evaluate. Did this campaign break even or profit? What would you change? Create a second campaign with adjustments.

---

## Advanced Notes

**Negative Keywords:** After a week, you might notice your ad is showing for irrelevant searches. Use negative keywords to exclude them. Example: If you're advertising paranormal paranormal romance and your ad shows for "paranormal psychology," add "psychology" as a negative keyword.

**Bid Adjustments:** In dynamic bidding, Amazon lowers your bid if the click seems unlikely to convert. In fixed bidding, you set a bid and stick with it. For beginners, dynamic is easier.

**Campaign Structure:** Once you've run one campaign, consider splitting by book or series. One campaign for Book 1, another for Book 1+2 bundle, another for your pen name as a whole. This lets you optimize separately.

**Budget Pacing:** If your daily budget is $10 and Amazon shows your ads to 1,000 people in 2 hours, you'll hit your budget fast and your ads disappear. Don't panic. This is normal. As you optimize, you'll spread impressions throughout the day.

## Metrics at a Glance

| Metric | What It Means | Healthy Range |
|---|---|---|
| CTR | % of people who clicked your ad | 0.5% - 3% |
| CPC | What you paid per click | $0.30 - $1.50 |
| ACoS | What you spent on ads vs. revenue | Under 30% is great; under 50% is okay |
| Conversions | Books sold from ad clicks | 10-30% of clicks |
| Impressions | Times your ad was shown | 100+ in first week (indicates engagement) |

---

## When to Scale Up

After running your first campaign for 2-4 weeks with solid data (good CTR, low ACoS, conversions happening), you're ready to:

1. Increase your daily budget by $5-10
2. Create a second campaign targeting different keywords
3. Test different ad copy on your existing campaign
4. Create campaigns for different books or series

AMS is a long game. Your first campaign is about learning. Your second campaign is about proving it works. Your third campaign is about scaling. Be patient with the process.
