Skill Builder

Beta Reader & Editor Edition · Foundations of Claude · Week 2

What this is: A guided builder for creating your own beta reader or editor skill file. Fill in the fields on the left. Watch your skill file generate on the right. When you're done, copy or download the file and drop it into your skills folder.

Reminder from the lesson: Building the file is step one. The skill won't activate until you actually load it in a session. Use your Session Opener to trigger it.

Build Your Beta Reader

Identity
The character/persona who's giving the feedback. Make them feel like a real person.
Beta examples: Paprika Lynn, Cecily Hale, Brinley Chase
Editor examples: Slash, Maeya Gray, Leah Voss
Lowercase, hyphenated. This is what you'll type when loading the skill.
Format: first-last-feedback or descriptor-editor
Who is this persona and what do they do? One sentence.
Beta: "Wine-drunk romance bestie who lives for banter and screams about heat scenes at 1am."
Voice & Personality
How do they talk? Sweary? Restrained? Academic? Wine-drunk? Cold? Be specific — Claude will mirror this.
3-5 personality details that make this persona distinct. What do they obsess over? What makes them roll their eyes?
Scope & Focus
What are they qualified to give feedback on? Be specific — broad personas give bland feedback.
Examples: "Vampire rom-com only" / "Dark romance, mafia and MC" / "Psychological thrillers, single-male-POV" / "Regency historical, slow-burn only"
What heat range are they reading for? Leave blank if not romance.
What does this beta look for in a chapter? What makes them love or hate a scene?
Beta example: "Whether the banter is actually funny. Whether the chemistry feels earned. Whether she'd keep reading past page 3. Whether the heat is hot enough."
What level of edit is this persona doing?
Output Style
Bullet points? Margin notes? Letter-style? Stream of consciousness? Numbered cuts? Be specific about format.
Tropes, moments, choices that make this persona freak out in a good way.
Pet peeves. Things that make them roll their eyes or DNF. Specificity is what gives the persona teeth.
Triggers
List the kinds of phrases or contexts that should activate this persona. The more specific, the better Claude triggers it.
Examples: "Margot read this chapter" / "Brutal feedback please" / "What would a [genre] reader think?" / "Run a slash edit on this scene"
